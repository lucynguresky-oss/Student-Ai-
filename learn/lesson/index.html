Cell Division & Mitosis

Cell division is the process by which a parent cell divides into two daughter cells. Mitosis produces two genetically identical daughter cells, while meiosis produces four genetically unique cells.

💡

Key fact: Mitosis occurs in somatic (body) cells, while meiosis occurs in reproductive cells to form gametes.

Diagram: Stages of Mitosis

The stages of mitosis are:

1. Prophase — Chromosomes condense and become visible. The nuclear envelope breaks down.

2. Metaphase — Chromosomes align at the cell equator (metaphase plate). Spindle fibers attach to centromeres.

3. Anaphase — Sister chromatids separate and are pulled to opposite poles.

4. Telophase — Nuclear envelopes reform around each set of chromosomes. Chromosomes begin to decondense.
